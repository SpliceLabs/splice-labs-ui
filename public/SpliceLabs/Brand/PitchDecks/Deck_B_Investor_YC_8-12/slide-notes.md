# DECK B — SPEAKER NOTES (ALL SLIDES)

## Slide 1 — Title
- Say who you are, what you build, in one breath. Don't elaborate yet.
- This slide should take <15 seconds. Move to the problem.

## Slide 2 — Problem
- Frame the problem as a market gap, not a complaint about existing players.
- "Retail-grade" means protocols that can't survive institutional due diligence.
- "TradFi bolt-ons" means banks tokenizing without understanding composability.
- The punchline: serious capital is waiting for serious infrastructure.

## Slide 3 — Solution
- Emphasize: we are builders, not investors or advisors.
- HELIOS is our core IP — the thing that's hardest to replicate.
- Cross-chain is table stakes; our edge is designing protocols that are natively multi-chain, not ported.
- "We build" is the line that separates us.

## Slide 4 — Product / How It Works
- Walk through the pipeline. This is a services + IP business model.
- The key differentiator is step 4: after deployment, HELIOS agents continue to operate and optimize the protocol. Most studios stop at step 3.
- Keep this visual. The diagram does the work.

## Slide 5 — Traction / Validation
- If we have hard traction, this is the most important slide. Lead with the biggest number.
- If traction is early, frame as "validation signals" — pipeline, LOIs, design engagements.
- Never overstate. Investors will verify. Credibility > impressiveness.
- Reference appendix case studies for depth.

## Slide 6 — Why We're Special / Insight
- This is the "why us, why now" slide. It's the intellectual core of the pitch.
- The insight is structural: the market needs builders, not launchers.
- HELIOS as a compounding asset is the defensibility argument.
- Keep this tight. One big idea, three supporting points.

## Slide 7 — Business Model
- Three revenue lines, each at a different time horizon. This de-risks the model.
- Engineering fees are the bread and butter today. HELIOS licensing is the scalable line.
- Token positions are aligned incentive — we only win if our builds succeed.
- Be prepared to answer: "What's your average deal size?" and "What's your gross margin?"

## Slide 8 — Market / Growth
- Size the market credibly. Bottom-up is better than top-down.
- If exact TAM data doesn't exist, build from: (# of institutional DeFi projects) × (average build cost) + (# of potential HELIOS deployments) × (license fee).
- Growth levers should be trends you can cite, not predictions you're making.

## Slide 9 — Team
- Founders only. No advisors, no extended team.
- Every line should answer: "Why is this person the right person to build this specific company?"
- If there's a prior exit, protocol deployment, or institutional relationship, lead with it.
- Keep it human. Investors invest in people.

## Slide 10 — The Ask
- Be specific. Vague asks lose credibility.
- Use of funds should map to milestones. "We need $X to achieve Y."
- Have your instrument decided before the meeting.
- End with contact info and a clear next step.
