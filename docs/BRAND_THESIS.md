# Splice Labs — Brand Thesis & Site Direction

## 1. What Splice Labs Is

Splice Labs is an **AI-first venture studio that builds AI-native companies**.

This is the load-bearing line of the entire brand. Everything else — the foundry framing, HELIOS, the project roster, the visual system — exists to substantiate this single claim. If a section of the site, a deck slide, or a piece of copy does not advance "AI-first studio building AI-native companies," it is taking up space that should be doing that work.

The studio operates at the intersection of AI and decentralized finance, but the wedge is not "we do crypto" or "we do AI." The wedge is **agent-native by default** — every company that comes out of the studio is built with agents as first-class operators, not bolted-on chatbots.

## 2. The Three Audiences

The marketing surface speaks to three audiences in parallel. A page that converts one and confuses the other two is a failure.

| Audience | What they need to feel in 10 seconds | What they need to see eventually |
|---|---|---|
| **Founders** (we want to attract) | "These people will accelerate me, not absorb me." | Operator credibility, residency / co-build path, HELIOS as inherited infra, portfolio outcomes. |
| **Investors** | "There is a productized moat here, not just a services shop." | HELIOS as platform IP, AI-native thesis, portfolio breadth, capital efficiency story. |
| **Clients / partners** | "These people ship working systems with audit posture from day one." | Prototype-first ethos, security-as-architecture, named projects, real founders. |

Today the site converges almost entirely on the client lane ("Request a private demo," "What you're building"). Founder and investor lanes are implicit. The next round of work has to surface those lanes without diluting the client one.

## 3. The Thesis Driving Site Design

> **Static marketing sites no longer differentiate.** A polished dark-mode landing page with a teal accent and a contact form looks like every other infra-studio site shipped in the last 24 months. To stand out — and to *prove* the AI-first claim rather than assert it — the site must be **interactive, generative, and visually narrative**.

A few corollaries that follow from this thesis:

1. **The medium is the message.** An AI-first studio that ships a static site is contradicting itself. The site itself should be a small, working demonstration of the studio's worldview: agent-native interactions, computed visual systems, surfaces that respond rather than display.
2. **Show, do not tell.** The site should not have a section titled "We are AI-first." It should have surfaces that could only have been built by an AI-first team. The visitor concludes the claim themselves, which is more durable than reading it.
3. **Interactive storytelling > linear scroll.** Each major section should give the visitor something to do, see resolve, or watch compute — not just paragraphs and bullet lists. Scroll is the chassis; interaction is the engine.
4. **Restraint still rules.** Interactivity is not maximalism. The brand's visual language — graphite + electric teal, mono labels, hairline borders, zero-radius — is the constraint that keeps the interactive layer from sliding into "crypto demo confetti."
5. **Every interaction earns its place.** Motion or interactivity that does not advance the AI-first / agent-native / venture-studio story is decorative noise and should be cut.

## 4. What Already Works

The current VersionD landing and BrandGuide get a lot right. Inventory before changing anything:

- **Visual restraint is a moat.** The graphite (#0E1114) + electric teal (#00D6A4) palette, hairline borders, zero-radius, Space Grotesk + Space Mono pairing, and the vertical "splice line" running the left gutter all read as a serious infra studio. This is hard to fake and hard to copy.
- **Voice is unhedged.** "Trust nothing. Audit everything." / "No mystery deployments." / "If it needs to be real, not theoretical, we can help." / "No spam. If it's not a fit, we'll say so." This voice converts the right audiences and repels the wrong ones — exactly what a studio needs.
- **Operator credibility is anchored.** The Founders section ("Built by Operators") plus Tim's Roost YC S14 + Stacks Foundation lineage reads as a founder-credible, investor-credible team rather than a marketing-led team.
- **HELIOS is a real platform anchor.** The four-layer stack diagram (Approval Gates → Policy Engine → Agent Orchestrator → Cross-Chain Runtime) gives both founders and investors something concrete to point at.
- **Anti-cliché posture.** The brand's prohibitions — no chains, coins, hexagons, shields, gradients on logo, drop shadows, "revolutionary," "disrupting" — are doing real work. They define the brand by negation in a category that desperately needs that.

## 5. What Is Missing or Underweighted

Measured against the thesis, the site has gaps:

1. **The AI-first claim is implicit, not load-bearing.** The hero says "Protocol and Systems Design. Prototype the Future." with "Agent-Native by Design." as a sub. The "AI-first venture studio building AI-native companies" frame is not actually on the page.
2. **The studio model is invisible.** The word "studio" does not appear. The "Foundry" framing reads as agency-led, not studio-led. Founders cannot tell if they should be applying to join, hiring Splice, or co-building.
3. **No founder lane.** No CTA, no residency / co-build path, no "bring us your idea" surface, no application form, no portfolio-as-portfolio.
4. **No investor lane.** No thesis page, no HELIOS-as-IP framing, no studio economics, no "why now" articulation.
5. **"Currently Building" is ambiguous.** Silent Markets / Anon Capital / Crown Futures could be client work, internal R&D, or portfolio companies. Investors and founders need this disambiguated; clients need to see it framed as proof of capability.
6. **The site is static.** The page is well-composed but it does not move, compute, or respond. For an AI-first studio, this is the central contradiction the next round of work must resolve.
7. **HELIOS is a section, not a demonstration.** It describes a control plane in prose. An AI-first studio's site should let the visitor *touch* the control plane, even minimally — a live agent action, a policy gate firing, a log streaming, anything that proves the claim.

## 6. Direction for the Next Round of Updates

The next iteration of the site should be evaluated against three questions, in order:

1. **Does it land "AI-first venture studio building AI-native companies" as the primary message?** If a visitor leaves with any other one-liner in their head, the site has failed its first job.
2. **Does it serve founders and investors as first-class audiences, not afterthoughts?** Distinct entry points, distinct CTAs, distinct proof.
3. **Is the site itself an artifact of an AI-first studio?** Does it move, compute, or respond in ways a static site cannot? Is interactivity earning its place by advancing the story rather than decorating it?

Concrete shapes the work might take (for discussion, not commitments):

- A hero that does something — generative, agent-driven, or live — instead of presenting a fixed headline.
- A live or simulated HELIOS surface where a visitor can watch an agent act, a policy gate decide, an audit log scroll.
- A studio model section that names the model: AI-first venture studio, what we co-build, what we keep, what founders get.
- A founder application or residency CTA that is its own surface, not buried in a generic contact form.
- An investor surface — thesis, HELIOS-as-IP, portfolio framing, contact path — distinct from the client demo flow.
- A portfolio section that is unambiguously a portfolio, with each company clearly tagged (studio-built, co-built, client engagement).
- Interactive moments throughout that demonstrate agent-native thinking — without sliding into demo-reel maximalism.

## 7. Non-Goals (To Stay Honest)

- **Not** a redesign of the visual system. The graphite + teal + mono + splice-line language is doing its job and should be preserved. Interactivity layers on top of it.
- **Not** a hype rebrand. No "revolutionary," no "disrupting," no chain / coin / hexagon imagery, no gradient logos. The voice and prohibitions stand.
- **Not** every section needs to be interactive. Restraint is still the brand. Pick the moments where interactivity earns the page.
- **Not** a generic "AI studio" pivot. The crypto / DeFi / agent-native specialization is the wedge. AI-first is the frame, not a replacement for the substance.

## 8. Working Definition for Decision-Making

For each proposed update, ask:

- Does this advance "AI-first venture studio building AI-native companies"?
- Which of the three audiences (founder / investor / client) does this serve, and is it a lane that is currently underserved?
- Is this surface doing something a static page cannot, in service of the story?
- Does it preserve the brand's restraint, voice, and visual rules?

If all four answers are yes, the update is on-thesis. If any are no, push back on the update before building it.
